Tonight you're drinking...

Chardonnay!

Wines made from the Chardonnay grape produce very different wines depending on where they're grown - in cool regions like Chablis, France, Chardonnays are citrusy and fresh, but when grown in Australia, they produce wines with tropical notes. We think that it's a great pairing for veggie dishes!

Eyes, nose, mouth

Like we've mentioned, Chardonnay is a versatile wine, and depending on where it's grown has very different qualities. You can expect to see a golden amber colour, in your glass. Smells and tastes include green fruits, like apple and pear, citrus (lime, lemon, grapefruit) and vegetable notes (cucumber), as well as tropical fruit notes like peach, banana, pineapple, and maybe even a hint of mango or fig.

You might also like to try Chardonnay with

It's great with vegetarian options, but this grape really compliments fish and chicken dishes, too, going well with tuna, salmon, prawns and and guineafowl. It also tastes great with creamy dishes, like pasta bakes and soups. Chardonnay pairs with Brie and Camembert cheeses and with custard based desserts.
