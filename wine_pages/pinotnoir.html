Pinot Noir!

A really versatile grape, Pinot Noir is our favourite wine for any chicken or poultry based dish. Most Pinot Noirs are best consumed while young and fresh, but you can get some really great aged ones, too.

Eyes, nose, mouth

Pinot Noirs have quite a clear, opaque colour for a red wine, and have a light red or garnet tinge. On the nose, you can expect to smell hints of red fruits such as strawberry, raspberry and cherry, while it also tastes very fruity and light, and low in acidity. You may notice a few vegetal notes, like mushroom.

You might also like to try Pinot Noir with

As we've pointed out, Pinot Noir is a really diverse grape. we would also recommend it with cold meats, pates and terrines, as well as mushroom risottos, cassoulets and rare steak. How about cheeses? Well, it really complements Brie and mild blue cheeses such as Gorgonzola and Stilton.
